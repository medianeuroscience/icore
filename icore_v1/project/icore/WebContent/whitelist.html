iCoRe - The GDELT Interface for Communication Research

Source Whitelist

Instead of querying all of the thousand sources monitored by GDELT, iCoRe is currently drawing on a specifically constructed, thoroughly validated, and extendable whitelist of 111 international, major news outlets. The rationale behind pre-selecting major news sources is to exclude rather insignificant sources for large-scale communication research (i.e., sources with a small audience) and to maintain a focus on news reporting, thereby excluding non-news websites (such as blogs or classified advertisements) that are also monitored by GDELT. In addition, for each source, iCoRe provides its country of origin, whether it is state run or not, and its political orientation (conservative, centrist, liberal) - information that GDELT does not provide. This information was gathered with the help of research assistants who collected information on a news outlet through the source’s website and online encyclopedias. iCoRe in turn allows the selection of articles from any of the 111 whitelisted sources, along with the stored metadata of a given news outlet. Furthermore, iCoRe currently only considers news outlets in English language or which features an English online version. Yet, in near future iCoRe will extend its list of major news sources by including non-English language sources using GDELT’s various translation capabilities.
